2 days at Balestrand - a small quaint fjord village on the Sognefjord.

We took the 1 hour boat ride from Flam across the Sognefjord to the small village of Balestrand. Balestrand is a quiet nice mix of cuteness and convenience, and, it's not over run by tourists! We highly recommend this town. Balestrand has outdoor activities for everyone, from dreamy fjordside strolls and strenuous mountain hikes (which we didn't do). The Bøyabreen Glacier is also nearby and offers a great tour (which we didn't do), and can be seen from the town.

The grand Kviknes Hotel presides over Balestrand's shoreline. It is the largest old wooden buildings in Norway. It was built in the 1870s and faces the rare little island in the fjord, which helped give the town its name: "Balestrand" means the strand or promenade in front of an island. Unfortunately, we did not stay at this hotel -- next time for certain! We spent one night and day at Balestrand, and it wasn't enough time to explore the local fjords and glaciers. Next time, we'll spend 2 or more days to see it all, and, we'll stay in the Kviknes Hotel.
