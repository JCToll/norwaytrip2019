Vigeland Sculpture Park

The Vigeland Sculpture park is in the Frogner Park with more than 200 sculptures by Gustav Vigeland (1869–1943) in bronze, granite and cast iron, including The Angry Boy (Sinnataggen in Norwegian), The Monolith (Monolitten) and The Wheel of Life (Livshjulet). The sculptures consist of naked human figures, in all variety of poses and situations – from the pastoral to the downright surreal – exploring the human form and human life, at its purest.Vigeland created the sculptures between 1924 and 1943, Vigeland was also responsible for the design and architectural outline of the Frogner Park, \w which is one of Norway's top tourist attractions. The park is open 24 hrs a day, and is free!

This was our favorite park in Oslo. In addition to the amazing sculptures, the Froger Park is worth exploring. This is a "must see" in Oslo. Just take the tram #12 from the downtown waterfront at Aker Brygge, and in 20 minutes you will be at the park.
