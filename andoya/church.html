Grandpa's Church in Dverberg

The Dverberg Church belongs to the Church of Norway (Lutheran denomination). The church was first built in 1589, and then after it was destroyed by lighting, it was rebuilt in 1843. This is an "octagonal church", the architecture of which is unique to Norway. Beginining in the 1680s, this style replaced the stave church style that had been prevalent in Norway. Torstein Raaby, the famous artic explorer, World War II resistance fighter, and Kon-Tiki expedition member, are buried in the church cemetery.

A story my Grandpa Hjalmar told, was that the first time he met my Grandmother Agnes, was during their confirmation class at this Dverberg church. Agnes lived on the other side of the island, somewhat far away back in the day, and attended a different church. But a couple times, the two confirmation classes met together at the Dverberg Church. Grandpa Hjalmor claims, he saw Agnes, and vowed he would marry that girl someday. Well,in his late teens, Hjalmar left Andøya and went to Oslo to attend culinary school, and he became a chef on ocean liners. After several years, in his mid-20's he immigrated to the USA, settling in Seattle. His vow to marry that girl was unfullfilled. One day, while vising relatives in Tacoma, he hopped on the downtown tram, and ran into Agnes, who had also immigrated to the USA. Well, the rest was history...
