Andøya Island

My heritage begins with my maternal grandparents, who were born and grew up on the island of Andøya, Norway.

Andøya is the northernmost island in the Vesterålen archipelago (69 deg N), situated about 300 kilometres (~200 miles) north of the Arctic circle. Andøya, is the 10th largest island in Norway, and is sparsly populated with only ~ 5,000 residents.

The map on the left shows the sites that we visited on Andøya. We started at Grandpa Hjalmar's homestead - this is where he was born, and grew up. The farm house still stands, and is lived in by Grandpa's nephew, Jan. The farm is located in the area known as Saura, just north of the small town of Dverberg, as shown on the map. Strange, enough, there is no address to the home, so it was difficult to find. It is just under 6 km north of the Dverberg grocery store. Just take the FV82 highway north past Dverberg, drive past the Dverberg Church which is on the right. Continue for a few minutes, and pass the road sign announcing "Saura" - which you won't find on Google Maps. Go past the Toyota dealer, on the left, which is in the middle of nowhere - strange. Then, you will soon see the white farmhouse with red trim, on the right, sitting far back from the road, over towards the fjord. See the pictures of the farm in the menu "Grandpa's Homestead."

The second day we drove counter-clockwise from the farm around the coast. We started driving up to Andenes, on the northern tip. Not much of a town, and not much to see. It's known for the whale boating tours, and in the winter, as THE place to view the Northern Lights. We then drove down to the Andoy Space Center, just minutes south of Andenes. We saw a great little film about the Aurora Borealus - Northern Light. There is a new space museum that is almost ready to open - look promising for our next visit. "

Soon after was the coastal village of Bleik - which has beautiful sandy white beaches. Pictures are shown below. Unfortunately, the rain and winds came, so we didn't get out for much of a walk. Driving further south, we passed Noss and then soon got to our Grandma's homestead, where she was born, and grew up. the pictures show that the house has been abandoned for years, but has recently been sold and is expected to be remodeled. We actually went to a cafe and farm called Nordtun Gård, which is owned by May, whose husband is my mother's cousin. Apparently this farm is on some of the farm land previously owned by my Grandma's family. "

Andøya is known for the numerous bog areas used for the extensive production of peat. Andøya is also well known for its fishing industry, and cloudberries. Besides the flat and largely continuous bog areas, Andøya also consists of steep mountain ranges reaching up to 700 metres (2,300 ft) high. The Andøya Space Center which was founded in 1962 is also located on the island. Since 1962, over 1,200 sounding rockets of all known configurations have been launched from this site. It's climate is between the subarctic and the subpolar oceanic climates, and yet is extremely mild despite its latitude because of the Gulf Stream influence. Summers are cool due to the Gulf Stream moderation, whereas even during the polar night period (winter), daytime highs frequently rise above freezing.
