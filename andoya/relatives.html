Andøya Relatives

Norway was devasted by World War II. The brutal German occupation of Norway during WWII is not well known by many. Our relatives were also greatly affected by this occupation. Shortly after the war ended, and Norway was freed, my maternal Grandparents, and mother and her sister, along with my Grandma's brother and family, traveled across the US to Detroit, where they purchased new automobiles, and then proceeded to New York City. They boarded a ship and ferried their car to Norway - this was the first commercial ship from the Americas to arrive in Norway after the war. They had provisioned essentials of food, clothes, medicine and more to bring to their relatives.

Upon arriving in Oslo, they drove North for several days to reach Andøya and their relatives. A couple of the following pictures show the families during this reunion. My mother spent 3 months in Andoya and learned to speak Norwegian. I can only image what a memorable experience this was for the families.
