Svolvaer - the Center of the Lofoten Islands

The Lofoten Islands are draped across the Norwegian Sea, far above the Arctic Circle. It is an archipelago of 6+ islands known for dramatic scenery of mountains and fjords. These pix are from the Lofoten islands,a couple hundred miles North of the Arctic Circle. This is a magical land of unbelievable mystical beauty - mountains, fjords, waterfalls, snow topped peaks down to verdant valleys spilling into countless fjords with rocky shores and even some cresent white sandy beaches with aquablue waters - almost Carribean-like. Many of the small towns are postcard perfect - shown in these pictures are the small towns of Svolvaer, Henningsvaer (fave), and Borg.

We made the city of Svolvaer the center of our adventures. Svolvaer is the largest city in the Lofoten Islands, and is located on the island of Austvågøya, along the Vestfjorden. The city is ideally located at the midpoint on the inner sea, facing across the Vestfjorden towards the mainland. The rain finally caught up with us during the 3 days, so we don't have as many pictures as I had hoped for.
