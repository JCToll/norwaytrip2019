Visit to Edvard Grieg's Home

Erik, who is the husband of my mother's cousin Berit, spent the day with us exploring the Edvard Grieg Museum at Troldhaugen, just south of Bergen. Erik was so kind to pick us up and to take us on this trip. He even brought coffee and lefsa to eat during our break. We really enjoyed our time with Erik, hearing the stories of his family, and about the year they spent in the USA while he attended Stanford University.

Edvard Grieg was a world famous Norwegian composer and pianist. He is widely considered one of the leading Romantic era composers during the late 1800s. His use and development of Norwegian folk music in his own compositions brought the music of Norway to international consciousness. His most famous composition is Peer Gynt (to hear some music go to: Peer Gynt ).

The Edvard Grieg Museum at Troldhaugen was the home of composer Edvard Grieg for 22 years. He composed many of his best-known works in the little garden hut, shown in the pictures. It is now a living museum comprising an exhibition centre with shop and cafe, concert hall, composers' cabin and Grieg's villa dating from 1885. We also had the opportunity to attend the daily private concert in concert hall.
