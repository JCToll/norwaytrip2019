Three Days In Bergen

Bergen is Norway’s second largest city, and is known as the 'city of 7 mountains', and as the city of islands, because some of the suburbs are on islands. The city is surrounded by mountains, islands, and fjords, including Sognefjord, the country’s longest and deepest. The downtown wharf is especially known for the Bryggen area, which features colorful wooden houses on the old wharf, once a center of the Hanseatic League's trading empire. This area was first developed in the 1500's.

We rented an Airbnb at the edge of the Bryggen buildings. A few block from our apartment, we took the Fløibanen Funicular which goes up Fløyen Mountain for panoramic views and hiking trails. See the pictures. Our second day, we went to Troldhaugen (troll's hill),which has the Edvard Grieg House where the renowned composer once lived. Erik, who is married to my mom's cousin, took us to the Edvard Grieg house, where we spent a wonderful afternoon exploring the house, and listening to a piano concert. This was followed by a trip to the Fantoft Stave Church, as shown in the pictures. The best part of our Bergen trip was meeting mom's cousin Berit and husband Erik, and their son and family. It was wonderful to hear their stories and meet the family, and have a delicous dinner as shown in the pictures. Finally, I would say that Bergen, like Oslo, is a 'must see'.
