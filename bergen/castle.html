Exploring the Bergenhus Fortress and Rosenkrantz Tower

The Bergenhus Fortress and the Rosenkrantz tower stand together at the entrance to the harbor. The fortress is the oldest and best preserved stone fortifications in Norway. It contains buildings dating as far back as the 1240s. The fortress is known for Haakon's Hall, constructed by King Håkon Håkonsson during the 13th century. Later, in the 1270s, the Rosenkrantz Tower was built near the fortress, to be a defensive tower.

I feel that this is a 'must see' - especially the Rosenkrantz Tower. The fortress is small, and really only contains the Haakon's Hall which is worth seeing. But the Rosenkrantz was a nightmare for a claustrophobic person like me. It contains dungeons, small dark rooms, narrow stone staircases that just go on forever -- all of this a very heart pounding experience for me being claustrophobic. The pictures can't capture the feeling of claustrophobia.
